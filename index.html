Introduction

Our research aims to analyze the post-COVID-19 changes in crime rates in Los Angeles. By employing data analysis techniques such as Geo-Mapping, bubble charts, radar plots, bar charts , line graphs and time series charts, we intend to visualize the areas with the highest crime rates and how they have evolved over time. We seek to understand the correlation between crime rates and factors such as crime type. This analysis will shed light on whether crime hotspots are influenced by demographic or income disparities. By examining crime details alongside recent events from 2018-2022, we aim to uncover compelling relationships and trends in crime rates and types during this period.

The global pandemic brought about significant shifts in people's daily routines, including the behaviors of those involved in criminal activities. Numerous studies have explored the pandemic's impact on crime rates, with findings indicating a notable decrease in crime across various developed countries. A broad overview suggests that, overall, the pandemic had a positive effect on crime, resulting in decreased crime rates. Explore our website and learn if the general overview is true for the city of Los Angeles.

We have 21 areas of interest in our dataset where the crime rates will be highlighted based on various factors. Those 21 areas are Wilshire, Central, Southwest, Van Nuys, Hollywood, Southeast, Newton, Mission, Rampart, Hollenbeck, West Valley, West LA, Olympic, Topanga, Northeast, 77th Street, Pacific, N Hollywood, Harbor, Foothill, Devonshire

About the Data

This dataset was collected from the National UFO Reporting Center.

Date

Sighting date reported (1973-2023). Data for 2023 was excluded as the year is still midway through.

Time

Specific time of sighting (by hour and minute).

City

US city of reported sighting.

State

US state of reported sighting.

Shape

Shape of observed UFO.

Duration

Duration of reported sighting. This variable was feature engineered from the "Time" variable.

Summary

A short description of the sighting given by the observer.

Images

A binary variable (Yes/No) describing whether the observer submitted an image of the reported sighting.
