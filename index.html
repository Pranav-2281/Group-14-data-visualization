Introduction

Our research aims to analyze the post-COVID-19 changes in crime rates in Los Angeles. By employing data analysis techniques such as Geo-Mapping, bubble charts, radar plots, bar charts , line graphs and time series charts, we intend to visualize the areas with the highest crime rates and how they have evolved over time. We seek to understand the correlation between crime rates and factors such as crime type. This analysis will shed light on whether crime hotspots are influenced by demographic or income disparities. By examining crime details alongside recent events from 2018-2022, we aim to uncover compelling relationships and trends in crime rates and types during this period.

The global pandemic brought about significant shifts in people's daily routines, including the behaviors of those involved in criminal activities. Numerous studies have explored the pandemic's impact on crime rates, with findings indicating a notable decrease in crime across various developed countries. A broad overview suggests that, overall, the pandemic had a positive effect on crime, resulting in decreased crime rates. Explore our website and learn if the general overview is true for the city of Los Angeles.

We have 21 areas of interest in our dataset where the crime rates will be highlighted based on various factors. Those 21 areas are Wilshire, Central, Southwest, Van Nuys, Hollywood, Southeast, Newton, Mission, Rampart, Hollenbeck, West Valley, West LA, Olympic, Topanga, Northeast, 77th Street, Pacific, N Hollywood, Harbor, Foothill, Devonshire



Why Los Angeles






Los Angeles (LA) stands as a unique urban landscape that attracts individuals from all walks of life, drawn by the allure of Hollywood and the promise of realizing their dreams. However, beneath the glitz and glamour lies a stark reality - many aspiring artists and individuals seeking fame and fortune find themselves facing disillusionment and hardship. The competitive nature of the entertainment industry, coupled with the high cost of living, often leads to dashed hopes and financial struggles.

As a consequence, a significant portion of the population in LA grapples with socio-economic challenges, including homelessness and poverty. The stark juxtaposition of wealth and destitution creates an environment ripe for various forms of criminal activity. Individuals who find themselves unable to achieve success through conventional means may turn to alternative paths, sometimes resorting to illicit activities as a means of survival or as an attempt to attain the lifestyle they desire.

Furthermore, the transient nature of LA's population, with people constantly moving in and out of the city in pursuit of their ambitions, contributes to the dynamic and complex nature of its crime landscape. Understanding the interplay between socio-economic factors, the entertainment industry, and criminal behavior is essential for comprehensively analyzing crime rates in Los Angeles.

By choosing to focus our project on studying crime rates in LA, we aim to unravel the intricate relationships between these factors and shed light on the underlying causes of crime in this vibrant yet multifaceted city. Through our analysis, we seek not only to gain insights into the patterns and trends of criminal activity but also to contribute to discussions on potential interventions and strategies aimed at addressing these issues and fostering a safer, more equitable community for all residents.

Geospatial Analysis

Spider Plot

Moving from the spatial to the temporal, Jack uses a spider plot to delve into the times when crimes are most likely to occur. This plot, vividly colored and comprehensive, displays the distribution of crimes across different times of day—Morning, Afternoon, Evening, and Night—across various police precincts such as Wilshire, Central, and Hollywood, among others. From the spider plot, Jack observes several key insights:-

Evening Peak: The majority of areas experience a significant uptick in crime during the evening hours, which suggests that increased patrols and community engagement initiatives during these hours might be effective.
Area-Specific Trends: While some areas like Central and Hollywood show a pronounced rise in evening crimes, others such as Van Nuys and Newton exhibit a more balanced distribution throughout the day. This variability necessitates area-specific policing strategies.
Comparative Analysis: The visual comparison across areas helps Jack evaluate the effectiveness of current policing strategies and understand where additional resources or new tactics are needed.

Line Chart

Jack scrutinizes a line graph depicting the occurrences of the top 10 crimes over three distinct periods: Pre-COVID, COVID, and Post-COVID. Here’s a breakdown of the trends from this graph:

1. Pre-COVID Period :- This period shows a relatively stable trend across most crime types with some variations. Notably, crimes like theft from motor vehicles and petty thefts are more prevalent compared to other crimes.
2. COVID Period :- During COVID, there is a significant dip in the number of occurrences for nearly all crime categories. This could be attributed to lockdown measures and less opportunity for crimes such as theft, which typically require the presence of victims in public spaces.
3. Post COVID Period :- Post-COVID shows a rebound in crime rates as restrictions ease. Interestingly, while some crimes have returned to pre-pandemic levels, others like battery and burglary from vehicles remain below their initial rates.

This analysis helps Chief Jack the Bull to get an insightful overview of how the crime landscape has evolved over the years, particularly highlighting the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. These insights are crucial for planning effective crime prevention strategies and ensuring a safer environment for the community in Los Angeles.
